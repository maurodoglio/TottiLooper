How to use TottiLooper

Getting started

  1. Click 🎤 Allow Microphone and grant permission when prompted.
  2. No microphone? Use the sample library to add built-in kick, snare and clap loops.
  3. If you have multiple audio inputs or are using an audio interface, choose the right Input and Channel.
  4. Plug in headphones — without them your mic will re-record whatever is already playing back.
  5. (Optional) Turn on Monitor input if you want to hear yourself live, then nudge Latency offset if your overdubs land a little early or late.
  6. (Optional) Set your BPM manually or with Tap Tempo, choose your Beats/bar, Beat unit, Subdivision and optional Loop bars, then enable Metronome, Count-in and Quantize to keep layered loops locked to the selected time signature.
  7. (Optional) Pick a Drum style and click 🥁 Generate drums to add a backing beat that follows your BPM.

Recording a loop

  1. Press ● REC (or ) to start. Watch the input meter to make sure your signal is healthy.
  2. Play your riff.
  3. Press ■ STOP (or the same shortcut again) when finished — or let Loop bars auto-stop exactly at the chosen bar count. The loop appears in the list and is ready to play.
  4. After your first loop, TottiLooper can suggest a matching BPM for the session — confirm it if the detection looks right.
  5. Press ■ DISCARD to abandon the current take without saving it.
  6. To punch in on an existing loop, enable Punch-in, choose the target loop and bar range, then record — the take is overdubbed onto that section instead of creating a new loop.

Per-loop controls

  • ▶ / ⏹ – start or stop this loop.
  • Key label – shows the loop's detected key, when TottiLooper can infer one.
  • 🔊 / 🔇 – mute or unmute.
  • S – solo this loop (everything not soloed is silenced).
  • L – set this loop as lead; other loops become quieter while the lead is playing (ducking).
  • ⇄ – play the loop in reverse.
  • ⬇ – download this loop as a WAV file.
  • ✕ – delete the loop. Use ↶ Undo / ↷ Redo or to step backward and forward through deletions.
  • ½× / 2× – toggle half-time or double-time playback for a loop.
  • Trim handles on the waveform – drag the left and right markers to tighten the loop without re-recording.
  • Vol / Pan / Low / Mid / High / Speed / Pitch / Fade In / Fade Out sliders – mix each loop, carve out frequency space with a 3-band EQ, time-stretch it, transpose it independently, and smooth loop boundaries.
  • Group selector – assign the loop to a named group for sub-mix control.
  • Follow tempo – opt a loop into BPM-following so tempo changes stretch it automatically.
  • Click or drag the waveform to scrub to a new playback position.
  • Click the loop name field to rename it (e.g. "Bass", "Chords", "Beatbox").

Loop groups

  • Click + Group to create a named sub-mix group (e.g. "Drums", "Vocals").
  • Use the Group selector on each loop card to assign loops to a group.
  • Each group has its own Vol slider, 🔊 Mute and S Solo controls that apply to all loops in the group.
  • Group volume multiplies each member loop's individual volume.
  • Soloing a group silences all loops outside that group; soloing a loop overrides groups.
  • Delete a group with ✕ — its loops move back to the ungrouped list.

Master controls

  • ▶ Play All / ■ Stop All – start or stop every loop at once.
  • Song mode – switch Play All into a bar-based song arrangement. Each loop gets its own Start and Bars timeline fields.
  • Now playing – shows the current bar and beat shared by the active loops.
  • Master slider – sets the overall output level.
  • Reverb slider – adds global room reverb to the master bus (0 = dry, 1 = fully wet send).
  • Compressor toggle – enables a dynamics compressor to glue layers and prevent clipping.
  • MIDI click – optionally export a click-track MIDI file at the current tempo for easy DAW alignment.
  • ⬇ Export – render the current mix (with all master bus effects) to a WAV file, and optionally download the matching MIDI click track alongside it.
  • 🥁 Generate drums – build a one-bar drum loop from the current BPM and selected style.
  • 🔗 Share – encode a small session into the page URL so you can send it as a link.
  • Scenes / setlist – save named snapshots of which loops are active plus every mixer setting, then fire them live with keys 1…9.
  • ✕ Clear All – remove every loop after confirming, with undo / redo support if you change your mind.

MIDI learn

  • Click Enable MIDI after plugging in your controller.
  • Use Learn on Record / Play All / Stop All, then press a pad or button.
  • Each loop card also has Learn toggle and Learn volume so you can map pads and faders per loop.

Scenes

  • Save stores the current set of playing loops plus mute / solo / volume / pan settings.
  • Trigger recalls that scene and crossfades to it over the selected 1–4 bar duration.

Keyboard shortcuts

Press 1…9 to trigger saved scenes 1–9 (or toggle loops 1–9 when that scene slot is empty).

    Customize shortcuts

    Click a field, then press the shortcut you want to use. Press Backspace or Delete to clear a binding.

    Gamepad / foot switch

    • If your browser exposes a connected controller via the Gamepad API, the first four buttons (indices 0–3) work hands-free.
    • Button 1 – start / stop recording
    • Button 2 – play all loops
    • Button 3 – stop all loops
    • Button 4 – toggle the next loop, cycling through your recorded loops

    Tips

    • Enable Quantize for tight, bar-aligned layers that never drift.
    • Use Song mode plus the per-loop timeline fields to sketch a verse / chorus arrangement without turning loops on and off by hand.
    • Enable Follow tempo on a loop before changing BPM if you want it to stay locked to the new tempo.
    • Set Loop bars to 1, 2, 4, or more when you want hands-free, bar-perfect takes.
    • Use Count-in so your first note isn't chopped off.
    • If a new loop may be in a clashing key, TottiLooper will warn you as soon as it finishes processing.
    • Drop Speed below 1× for half-time pads, push above 1× for chipmunk leads.
    • Use Pitch to transpose loops up or down without changing tempo.
    • Use Pan to spread rhythm and lead parts across the stereo field — it instantly sounds bigger.
