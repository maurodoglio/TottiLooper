How to use TottiLooper

Getting started

  1. Click 🎤 Allow Microphone and grant permission when prompted.
  2. If you have multiple audio inputs or are using an audio interface, choose the right Input and Channel.
  3. Plug in headphones — without them your mic will re-record whatever is already playing back.
  4. (Optional) Turn on Monitor input if you want to hear yourself live, then nudge Latency offset if your overdubs land a little early or late.
  5. (Optional) Set your BPM, enable Metronome, Count-in and Quantize to keep layered loops locked to a bar.

Recording a loop

  1. Press ● REC (or ) to start. Watch the input meter to make sure your signal is healthy.
  2. Play your riff.
  3. Press ■ STOP (or the same shortcut again) when finished — the loop appears in the list and is ready to play.
  4. Press ■ DISCARD to abandon the current take without saving it.

Per-loop controls

  • ▶ / ⏹ – start or stop this loop.
  • 🔊 / 🔇 – mute or unmute.
  • S – solo this loop (everything not soloed is silenced).
  • ⇄ – play the loop in reverse.
  • ⬇ – download this loop as a WAV file.
  • ✕ – delete the loop. Use ↶ Undo or to bring it back.
  • Vol / Pan / Speed sliders – mix each loop and warp its pitch via playback rate.
  • Click the loop name field to rename it (e.g. "Bass", "Chords", "Beatbox").

Master controls

  • ▶ Play All / ■ Stop All – start or stop every loop at once.
  • Master slider – sets the overall output level.
  • MIDI click – optionally export a click-track MIDI file at the current tempo for easy DAW alignment.
  • ⬇ Export – render the current mix to a WAV file, and optionally download the matching MIDI click track alongside it.
  • 🔗 Share – encode a small session into the page URL so you can send it as a link.

MIDI learn

  • Click Enable MIDI after plugging in your controller.
  • Use Learn on Record / Play All / Stop All, then press a pad or button.
  • Each loop card also has Learn toggle and Learn volume so you can map pads and faders per loop.

Keyboard shortcuts

    Customize shortcuts

    Click a field, then press the shortcut you want to use. Press Backspace or Delete to clear a binding.

    Gamepad / foot switch

    • If your browser exposes a connected controller via the Gamepad API, the first four buttons (indices 0–3) work hands-free.
    • Button 1 – start / stop recording
    • Button 2 – play all loops
    • Button 3 – stop all loops
    • Button 4 – toggle the next loop, cycling through your recorded loops

    Tips

    • Enable Quantize for tight, bar-aligned layers that never drift.
    • Use Count-in so your first note isn't chopped off.
    • Drop Speed below 1× for half-time pads, push above 1× for chipmunk leads.
    • Use Pan to spread rhythm and lead parts across the stereo field — it instantly sounds bigger.
